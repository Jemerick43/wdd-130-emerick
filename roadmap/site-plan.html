Overview

Purpose

To be honest, we live in a virtual world. Almost all information is available to anyone with a phone. But we all know that one relative who still lives in the 20th century; that one person who has no idea what the “internet” is or what it can do. Wouldn’t it be nice if there were a bootcamp for those who are taking their first virtual steps? Well, that’s the purpose of my website; to orient those who tend to become “dis”-oriented on the web.

Audience

If you or a loved one have ever struggled to understand what the internet is, or HOW to navigate/use this powerful and massive resource, this site is for you!

Content

Home page

People will come to this site to learn about the internet. Visitors will learn what the internet is, about good web-safe privacy standards, about what the internet has to offer, and how to more effectively use a web-browser. Visitors will also learn about information their own computer can provide others across the internet, what processes are automatically performed upon visiting a website, and the risks associated with such. Visitors will be treated to multiple online activities to teach concepts and ideas which are not readily shown through normal web activity. (Ideas such as web privacy, net neutrality, and data collection will all be shared and taught in a simple manner).

Images for the Home page

Safe Surfing

This page will cover the basics of Web privacy and different solutions to the problem of the data industry. Ideas discussed will include new web-browsers (such as Duck Duck Go), the use of a VPN, and new and upcoming technology to secure all data online (the "For Privacy" project). Page will include FAQ's about internet safety such as: "Is my personal information secure on the internet?", "Who has access to see what is on my computer", "Is 'hacking' as hard as the movies make it seem?", and so on.

Images for the Page 2

Thievery Online

This page will be devoted to the topics of data collection and advertisment. Visitors will discover the concepts of targeted advertisment as well as "packet priority" with regards to the net neutrality debate. Companies such as Google, Apple, Facebook, IBM, Microsoft, and others will be displayed with data related to revenue related to data collection and how the individual visiting my site plays into those statistics.
